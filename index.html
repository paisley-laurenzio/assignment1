Though having live subjects can pose many challenges I love the difficulty of capturing emotion and personality in my subjects and connecting with them in a way that they feel comfortable and I am able to capture their true character.

How to Capture Emotion and Personality

It is extremely difficult to capture a natural expression that shows the true personality of your subject. It requires a connection between the photographer and the subject on a level that breaks a barrier and allows the subject to become entirely confortable. The worst nightmare for photographers when photographing people is when the expression is obviously forced.

There are several things that you as a photographer can do to capture the true personality of your subject. It is important that you are confident and comfortable with a clear idea of what you want. If you are comfortable and able to direct your subject confidently your subject will feel much more comfortable, opening up and revealing their true personality. You have to be prepared to spend the first part of a photo shoot breaking in your subject until you break the subject’s barrier and are able to capture their comfortable natural character. It is important to understand how to reach this level of comfort with your subject. With children it is often easiest to distract them with fun stories and to get them talking. For more mature subjects they will often feel most comfortable when you confidently direct, telling them how to stand and what facial expression you want.
